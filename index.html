üìö Selected Papers
CityEL: A web-based platform to support city-scale building energy efficiency based on AutoBPS [PDF]
Chengcheng Song, Jingjing Yang, Zhiyuan Wang, Ruoheng Li, Xiufeng Pang, Yixing Chen*
Sustainable Cities and Society, 2025. (JCR Q1, IF=10.5)

CityEL (City Energy Lab) is a web-based platform for urban building energy efficiency. Unlike traditional UBEM tools, it focuses on rapid modeling and neighborhood-scale analysis, using GIS and AutoBPS to streamline energy benchmarking, retrofit evaluation, and cost analysis.

Developing urban building energy models for shanghai city with multi-source open data [PDF]
Chengcheng Song, Zhang Deng, Wenxian Zhao, Yue Yuan, Mengyue Liu, Shen Xu, Yixing Chen*
Sustainable Cities and Society , 2024. (JCR Q1, IF=10.5)

This study integrates open data to model Shanghai's building energy use, covering 609,000 footprints. Using machine learning, 63 building prototypes were classified with 95% accuracy, and energy simulations aligned with benchmarks. The approach improves urban energy modeling for sustainability planning.

An emergency aircraft evacuation simulation considering passenger overtaking and luggage retrieval [PDF]
Chengcheng Song, Quan Shao*, Pei Zhu, Min Dong, Wenfei Yu
Reliability Engineering & System Safety, 2023. (JCR Q1, IF=9.4)

This study models cabin evacuation with cellular automata, analyzing how luggage retrieval and overtaking behaviors impact efficiency. Results show luggage retrieval hinders evacuation most, but effective crew guidance can improve safety.
